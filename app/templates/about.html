About Alum

A private social network for every American high school — built to reconnect graduates with the friends, classmates, and mentors they lost track of after commencement.

A year after graduating from my high school, York Prep, a small independent school on Manhattan's Upper West Side, I realized I had no idea where most of my classmates had landed, what they were studying, what they were working toward, or how to reach them if I tried. I also had no clue about past students, as York had no alumni database or footprint on other networking sites. Though York notoriously sent newsletters to alumni, graduates rarely ever checked them, and the closest thing to a directory was a spreadsheet that had been out of date since before we left.

I built the first version of Alum as my senior year capstone project, which was, at the time, an exercise without a working website. It wasn't anything serious the following year, but when I began prepping for investment banking recruiting at Baruch and watched peers from larger schools pull ahead due to the simple fact that they could pick up the phone and reach a graduate from their high school who had walked the same hallway five years earlier, the idea sparked in my head once again. This gap, multiplied across the thousands of high schools in this country, is what Alum was built to close.

Alum serves three types of people, with each demographic benefiting immensely. On the site, alumni get their network back, the friends they lost track of after graduation, and the wider class of people who shared four formative and unforgettable years with them. Current students get direct access to the kind of casual mentorship that large schools have always provided through databases, only now being available to any student whose school is on the platform. For example, a junior in high school considering pre-med can message a graduate in residency. A senior who sat in an alumni's exact desk three years ago and made it through can pick up the phone and request a chat. Regarding the schools themselves, Alum will replace the nittygritty work of spreadsheets and dead email lists that front offices have relied on for decades, offering a single isolated platform for records, announcements, fundraising, and alumni engagement.

We're a small team based in New York, working with our first group of independent schools across the city, and we're building Alum on the belief that the graduates of every high school already share something most communities never get to build. They're simply not in touch.
