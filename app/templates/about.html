About Alum

A private social network for every American high school — built to reconnect graduates with the friends, classmates, and mentors they lost track of after commencement.

Alum started with a question that I couldn't answer. A year after graduating from my high school, York Prep, a small independent school on Manhattan's Upper West Side, I realized I had no idea where most of my classmates had landed, what they were studying, what they were working toward, or how to reach them if I tried. I also had no clue about past students, as York had no alumni database or footprint on other networking sites. The school sent newsletters to email accounts none of us checked anymore, and the closest thing to a directory was a spreadsheet that had been out of date since before we left.

I built the first version of Alum as my senior-year capstone project, mostly as an exercise without a working website. It became something more serious the following year, when I was preparing for investment banking recruiting at Baruch and watched peers from larger schools pull ahead due to the simple fact that they could pick up the phone and reach a graduate from their high school who had walked the same hallway five years earlier. I couldn't. Almost no one I went to school with could. That gap, multiplied across the thousands of high schools in this country, is what Alum was built to close.

Alum serves three groups, and each of them gains something the current arrangement quietly takes away. Graduates get their network back, the friends they lost track of after graduation, and the wider class of people who shared four formative years with them. Current students get something even better, which is direct access to the kind of casual mentorship that wealthy schools have always provided through informal channels and databases, now available to any student whose school is on the platform. A junior considering pre-med can message a graduate in residency. A nervous senior can hear from someone who sat in her exact desk three years ago and made it through. And for schools themselves, Alum replaces the patchwork of spreadsheets and dead email lists that development offices have relied on for decades, offering a single branded dashboard for alumni records, announcements, fundraising, and real engagement data.

We're a small team based in New York, working with our first cohort of partner schools across the city, and we're building Alum on the belief that the graduates of every American high school already share something most communities never get to build. They're simply not in touch. That's a solvable problem.
